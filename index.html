Key Figures

  • mehmed-ii — The Conqueror himself: biography, campaigns, character, and legacy
  • bayezid-ii — The Conqueror’s son, who consolidated the empire after Mehmed’s death and managed the Cem Sultan crisis
  • ahmed-iii — The Tulip Age sultan (r. 1703–1730), patron of reform literature, whose reign saw cultural efflorescence and the first Ottoman printing press
  • mustafa-ii — Sultan (r. 1695–1703) who personally led campaigns against the Habsburgs and was deposed in the Edirne Vaq’ası after the disaster at Zenta
  • suleiman-the-magnificent — Suleiman I, the tenth Ottoman sultan, legislator, and peak of Ottoman power
  • selim-i — Selim the Grim, conqueror of Egypt and Syria, who transformed the state into an Islamic caliphate
  • sokollu-mehmed-pasha — The Bosnian grand vizier who ran the empire under Selim II, builder of the Višegrad bridge
  • barbarossa-hayreddin-pasha — The great Ottoman admiral, victor of Preveza, who dominated the Mediterranean for Suleiman
  • osman-i — The founder of the Ottoman dynasty, ghazi frontier leader who established the beylik at Söğüt
  • orhan-i — The second Ottoman ruler, who captured Bursa, created the first standing army, and crossed into Europe at Gallipoli
  • mahmud-pasha — Grand Vizier of Mehmed II, commander of the major campaigns of 1456–1466
  • gedik-ahmed-pasha — Janissary general who rose from the ranks, served Mehmed II and Bayezid, and was executed in 1482
  • cem-sultan — Mehmed II’s younger son, whose imprisonment by the Hospitallers became a major diplomatic crisis
  • zaganos-pasha — Zağanos Pasha, Mehmed II’s trusted vizier and fleet commander, key figure in the conquest of Constantinople and the early campaigns
  • constantine-xi — Constantine XI Palaiologos, the last Byzantine Emperor, who fell defending Constantinople in 1453
  • john-hunyadi — John Hunyadi, Hungarian regent and military commander, victor at the Siege of Belgrade (1456)
  • matthias-corvinus — Matthias Corvinus, King of Hungary, opponent of Mehmed II in Bosnia, builder of the Black Army
  • david-comnenus — David Comnenus, the last Emperor of Trebizond, deposed and executed by Mehmed II in 1463
  • vlad-the-impaler — Vlad III Dracula, voivode of Wallachia, whose rebellion against Mehmed in 1462 led to the night attack and Ottoman invasion
  • skanderbeg — George Kastrioti Skanderbeg, leader of the Albanian revolt against Ottoman rule in the 1460s
  • uzun-hasan — Uzun Hasan, ruler of the Aq Qoyunlu confederation, defeated by Mehmed at the Battle of Otlukbeli (1473)
  • demetrios-palaiologos — Demetrios Palaiologos, Despot of the Morea, whose invitation to Mehmed II led to the Ottoman conquest of the Peloponnese
  • thomas-palaiologos — Thomas Palaiologos, Despot of the Morea, rival of Demetrios, who fled to Rome after the Ottoman conquest
  • sari-mehmed-pasha — Sarı Mehmed Pasha, seven-time chief defterdar under Ahmed III, author of the reformist Kitab-i Güldeste (Naṣā’iḥ ül-vüzera)
  • koci-bey — Koçi Bey, 17th-century palace official and author of the most influential Ottoman reform treatise, the Risale (c. 1630)
